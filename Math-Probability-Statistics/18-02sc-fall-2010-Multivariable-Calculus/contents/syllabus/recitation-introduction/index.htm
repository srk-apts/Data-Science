Search Tips
Exclude words from your search
Put - in front of a word you want to leave out. For example, jaguar speed -car

Search for an exact match
Put a word or phrase inside quotes. For example, "tallest building".

Search for wildcards or unknown words
Put a * in your word or phrase where you want to leave a placeholder. For example, "largest * in the world".

Search within a range of numbers
Put .. between two numbers. For example, camera $50..$100.

Combine searches
Put "OR" between each search query. For example, marathon OR race.

JOEL LEWIS: Hi. My name is Joel Lewis. At the time of this recording in 2010 I'm a graduate student in the Math Department at MIT, and I'm also a TA for OCW Scholar 18.01 and 18.02.

CHRISTINE BREINER: Hi. I'm Christine Breiner. I'm a Moore Instructor here at MIT and for OCW Scholar, I'll be a teaching assistant for 18.01 and for 18.02.

DAVID JORDAN: My name is David Jordan and I'm a graduate student here at MIT. And I'm a TA for OpenCourseWare Scholar 18.02.

So I thought I would say a little bit about what we do with recitations here at MIT. So, the purpose of the recitations is for you to apply what you've learned in lecture towards solving specific concrete problems and then for you to compare your solutions to those problems with those of your TA.

CHRISTINE BREINER: The purpose of the recitations on OCW Scholar is really the same purpose that David described for recitations here at MIT. What we want you to do is have an opportunity to work varying levels of problems and then see us give detailed solutions to those problems so that you can see how we think about mathematics.

JOEL LEWIS: Almost all of our videos have a natural moment where you can pause after the problem's been stated. And we really strongly suggest you work out the problem on a piece of paper before going back to the video and seeing our solutions. This will help you do mathematics rather than be able just to see mathematics.

Hope you enjoy the course.
